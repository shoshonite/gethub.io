Bradley Redden

I am a solutions-oriented leader with a BSc in Geology from Saint Mary's University and a broad background spanning mineral exploration, municipal government, and environmental policy. I served four years as an elected Councillor and two years as Deputy Warden for the Municipality of Annapolis County, where I worked closely with planning staff, engaged the public on land-use and coastal protection policy, and helped rebuild inter-municipal relationships across the Annapolis Valley. My geological training and political experience have sharpened my ability to bridge technical science and practical policy — synthesizing complex information from scientific, economic, and social sources into decisions that matter. I am deeply committed to climate adaptation and nature-based solutions, with particular interest in the coastal ecosystems of the Upper Bay of Fundy. I am currently completing a certificate in Geospatial Data Analytics at NSCC's Centre of Geographic Sciences. Read my reflections on my COGS experience.
